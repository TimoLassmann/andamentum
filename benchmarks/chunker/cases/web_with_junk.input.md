# Headline: Cell-free DNA test predicts cancer recurrence

By Staff Writer · 4 min read · Updated yesterday

A new blood test that detects fragments of tumor DNA can reliably predict whether breast cancer is likely to come back, according to a study published this week in Nature Medicine. The test, called SignaturaCDx, looks at patterns of DNA methylation in cell-free fragments shed by dying tumor cells.

In a cohort of 412 women with early-stage breast cancer, the test correctly identified 87% of those who later relapsed, with a false-positive rate of just 6%. This compares favorably to standard imaging, which has a sensitivity of around 60% in the same patient population.

The researchers, based at the University of Edinburgh and the Wellcome Sanger Institute, plan to validate the test in a larger trial of 5,000 patients starting next year. Lead author Dr. Sara Mitchell said the team hopes the test will eventually allow oncologists to escalate treatment for high-risk patients before recurrence becomes detectable on scans.
